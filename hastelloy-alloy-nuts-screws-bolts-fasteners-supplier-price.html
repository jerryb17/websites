Hastelloy Screws

Hastelloy Fasteners are manufactured from hastelloy, which is considered to be a high- performance alloy or a superalloy, by the metallurgical industry. As such, Hastelloy Bolts Supplier which are used in severe corrosion environs. At elevated temperatures, Hastelloy Set Screws exhibit excellent mechanical properties. Hastelloy Flat Washer are used in applications that involve the use of hypochlorites, formic acid, acetic acid and various solvents. Hastelloy lock washer is highly resistant to corrosion and so they and Hastelloy nuts are used in various industries such as flue gas desulfurization systems, pulp & paper bleaching and digesters, etc.

Regal Sales Corporation is EIL approved supplier of Hastelloy Fasteners to USA, UK, Russia, Malaysia, Oman, Egypt, Bahrain, France, Sweden, Indonesia, Iran, Germany, Italy, Qatar, Turkey, Thailand, Kuwait, Canada, Australia, Sri Lanka, Saudi Arabia, Singapore, Israel and UAE.

Nickel is a primary alloy that is used by Regal Sales Corporation in the manufacture of hastelloy fasteners. But, in manufacturing of hastelloy threaded rod, other elements are also added such as iron, manganese, aluminum, molybdenum, zirconium, chromium, tungsten, copper, cobalt, titanium and carbon. Because of these properties, hastelloy anchor bolts, are used in applications and industries that involve the use of phosphoric acid, wet chloride gas, acetic anhydride, chlorine solutions, chlorides, etc. Hastelloy studs have the capability to resist extremely harsh chemicals, including the likes of hydrochloric acid and sulfuric acid.

Hastelloy u-bolts display superb corrosion resistance and so they are used in off-shore oil and down-hole environs, chemical processes and oil & gas sour gas services. One of the main reasons why there is such a global demand for hastelloy screws, is due to the fact that they offer a high-stress service and they can very effectively survive in high- temperature environs. Also, hastelloy nuts and bolts can survive in environs that are prone to erosion and/or very highly corrosive environs, where other inexpensive iron-based alloys and more common iron-based alloys would not succeed, such as the likes of distillation equipment, nuclear reactors pressure vessels, chemical industry valves and pipes, chemical reactors, etc.
