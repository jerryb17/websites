Monel Nuts and Bolts

Monel Fasteners are nickel-copper alloy (about 67% Ni – 23% Cu) that is resistant to sea water and steam at high temperatures as well as to salt and caustic solutions. Monel Bolts is a solid solution alloy that can only be hardened by cold working. Monel Stud Bolts have nickel alloy exhibits characteristics like good corrosion resistance, good weldability and high strength. A low corrosion rate in rapidly flowing brackish or seawater combined with excellent resistance to stress-corrosion cracking in most freshwaters, and Monel Alloy Bolts resistance to a variety of corrosive conditions led to its wide use in marine applications and other non-oxidizing chloride solutions. Monel Hex Nuts are nickel alloy is particularly resistant to hydrochloric and hydrofluoric acids when they are de-aerated. As would be expected from its high copper content, alloy Screw is rapidly attacked by nitric acid and ammonia systems.

Monel Studs has great mechanical properties at subzero temperatures, can be used in temperatures up to 1000° F, and its melting point is 2370-2460° F. However, monel is low in strength in the annealed condition so, a variety of tempers may be used to increase the strength.
